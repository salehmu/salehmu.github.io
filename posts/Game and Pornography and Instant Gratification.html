Game and Pornography and Instant Gratification

I'm quite sure that "games" in the title might seem a quite subjective or unrelated word, at least doesn't feel like a substitute or a par for the obscenity of watching porn, people don't usually judge someone who is being identified as a 'gamer' a lot. For me, I believe that the costs of both are the same and each side is worse than the other one (maybe porn is worst because it fucks you up as well (and more important) the people around you more than games do). I'm going to explain this about video games because you usually have the regular objection "video games are just fun" [if you have an objection about porn then you have problems to solve bro].

Whenever I define video games as a type of instant gratification, social engineering and mind washing method, people start to consider me as the zealous regicide nerd who doesn't want to waste his time, this happened 2 years ago when a major update was unleashed in the Linux kernel (I think it was supposed to make gaming easier), and I'm really not (at least this is not my self-image). I can build my argument against video games in 3 correlated points: 1. addiction. 2. (sigh) time. 3. the semi-delayed instant gratification, the third point is the most crucial to me.

I believe that gaming is a very fine art, in narrative game story you may find plots and values that you will not find in a lot of literature books. We all know a lot of games that have been turned into books and movies because of how much ingenuine it was. And this encounter the first 2 points. Games need a lot too much time than a movie, novel, even sometimes more than a TV show (which I also have concerns about) to rummage the values inside the game story. For me this is an adequate reason to quit.

The most critical cost of video games which makes it more dangerous for people (especially men) than porn, is how manipulative they are. Here I'm not talking about the insidious game design that makes you addict it (login twice a day, and you will get more PX!) the but how it generates fake-stimulated achievement, that is the leveling up. Pornography doesn't do the same. It's only a stimulated intimate and instant joy, its gratification mostly ends at the moment you're getting to wash up, you might feel guilt about it, and you probably know already that it is a bad thing, this is not the case with video games.

The way that most of video games are designed is that they're based on the concept of leveling up, which a very crucial concept in real-live. Conquer challenges and quests is the most joyous thing in the real life for a man who is doing something in his life. Just imagine how fun would it be if you live wild, gets your needs by your hands, the more you train and polish your self, the more you get gratifications (food, house, exp or whatever). This is what life have always been about. I think most religions can relate to that as well. This delayed-gratification is what makes the most of life, the noble values that you're standing for, everything that you're considering as magnanimity does mostly intersect with the values of delayed-gratification, everything that is malignant doesn't (think about it).

We do need the delayed-gratification in our life to actually accomplish something. This is what 'loser' mostly can't do, sorry for using such a term but since this is informal blog it doesn't matter, the loser can't bear delayed-gratifications, guess what differs two people who live in shitty circumstances, but one passes and other suffers? the other chose to suffer.

Now the problem with video games is that it have this kind of delayed-gratification. We as humans have that predilection of achievements especially the delayed ones which makes the most of video games, you're going wild, train, kill some people or do some missions, build good gear and challenge yourself more and more. This is literally what your brain would love to do in the real life but here is the thing: the leveling up is way more comfortable that it is in the real life, but even maybe more adventurous. Gradually your brain is going to love this kind of gratification, you don't have to make a real-life achievements anymore to feel good because you're a master in some online games. Maybe I'm being drastic about it, but ask yourself, how many times have you met an obsessed gamer who have minimum achievements in his/her life? None? I'm sorry for saying that but the most underdogs people I've ever seen are gamers.
