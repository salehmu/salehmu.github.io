On Social Media Neoconditioning or Why Redditor is a NPC?

2021-08-04
Articles
Media , Psychology

I hope I’m not updogging the definition of “NPCs” but I think that nothing can explain what does it mean (even what the words that it stands for) more than reddit users or the so-called, “the redditors” actually I think that designation supports the argument I’m about to make.

Classical Conditioning

This is not a science post, but I’ve to declare some terms, the most important one to me right now is “conditioning”, it’s a subdivision of big scoop called behaviorism, some scientists don’t consider conditioning or behaviorism as a real ‘science’ (which doesn’t mean they treat it as pseudoscience, It is an epistemological problem) but it’s very empirical anyway.

There are two main types of conditioning, classical conditioning, and operant conditioning, you perhaps have heard about Pavlov’s dog. Ivan Pavlov was a deft Russian physiologist, his experiment is not very unparalleled in classical conditioning, but you can say it was exclusive to the psychology communities then.

Pavlov redirected a dog’s fluids to measure how the digestive is influenced by any external tutelage. In short, the experience was to bring food to the restrained dogs every day, and study how much saliva was produced when the worker who brought the food came for them. Day after day, Pavlov began to observe what he called the Psychic secretion, the classical condition pivot: an anticipatory salivation happened to the dogs, it was at first like salivation happens when the food door is opened, and it ends with the dogs' digestive worked to bring out saliva as soon as the dogs hear the sound of the worker’s feet coming. Pavlov’s dog experiment

Indeed, it’s an interesting experiment, it may seem outdated to many psychologists today, but I think it is a very appropriate introduction to talk about conditioning in general, especially operant conditioning, which is the main method behind social media websites.

Operant conditioning

Operant conditioning is not much different from classic in its core. Approximately, we can tell that the main difference is about the behavior, nevertheless, the classical condition modifies learning procedure behavior (like saliva in the main experiment), operant can modify the behavior itself.

Cursorially, BF Skinner managed to change/create some new behaviors when some actions occur to a mouth. For example, after exposing a mouse to electrical charge when it hears the sound of sea waves for serval times, the mouse involuntarily develops a new behavior of running and take fright just to hear the sound of sea waves.

BF Skinner’s mouse

Some might see that this is a very outdated definition, there are many developments in this definition today, some scientists have discovered that this behavior may be passed on to later generations, and some have created new forms of operant conditioning like Richard Thaler’s libertarian paternalism, which is a simply conditioning, but the lib mice think that they are the ones who chose how to behave, it is a common social engineering method most social media sites use it.

Social Media Social Engineering

If you’ve used any social media site, you perhaps have been conditioned already, and you’re going to be a “redditor” very soon. Social media sites have their own rewards aka likes/upvotes/shares/etc.. And this is not necessarily bad for you it might give you nice feelings that people love your posts, but this is not the bad part, actually this part may occur in real life when someone tells you that he likes your words.

The problem is with the other parts that social media use to social engineer you, the punishment and alcoholism parts. Alcoholism happens when you really care about reactions in your posts, you will sooner or later seek to have more likes in your posts till point you will be ready to change your own views to be the same as the others who get more likes than you. This is bad, but anyway, if you’re already a jerk to pay attention to some virtual reacts, social media social engineering won’t make it worse.

NPCs

The awful part is the punishment, not only can this part change all idiots' thoughts and make them think the same way, but it can also affect smart people and turn them into NPC’s or “redditors”. Many of these sites didn’t have a way to punish, in fact, they didn’t have rewards in the beginning (until Zock created likes), if I disagree with your post then, I won’t be able to punish you, the most I can do is argue with you in the comments, now there are many ways like quoted tweets, HAHA reactions on Facebook, youtube dislikes, and worst of all: reddit downvotes.

Reddit Herd Creation Methodology

Unlike Facebook and Twitter, the content shown on Reddit is strictly governed by what the majority sees as right or wrong (that’s a shame if you ask me), if someone makes a new post and gets a third downvote when it’s posted, they will probably never see it again [because Reddit It has a minimum amount of karma to keep posts “unhidden”, plus most users ask Reddit to automatically sort content for them by upvotes], the worst part is the discussions, having many downvotes will completely hide your comment, and of course, it will be embarrassing for commoners to get into a conversation with someone and get many downvotes while the other gets hundreds of upvotes and rewards, what happens, in the end, is that the person deletes his comment, enter any old argument between two people on Reddit, and you will most likely find that comment with a lot of downvotes is deleted by the user, redditors would rather delete their thoughts and not express them than get downvotes!

Actually, it became a meme there to ask “How the hack is the user deleted?! He is everywhere”, deleted comments by users became very ubiquitous till some people really think that there is a user named deleted comment with “deleted” word.

It’s the same, and it might actually happen on any other social media site, however, I’ve never seen retards like the retards on Reddit, they are all a part from the same person, even in ‘intellectual’ conversational subreddits, they’re all the same person because any new idea that will appear, which is opposed to the ideas of the herd or even a part of them, will be suppressed and will either disappear or be altogether eliminated by its owner.

Of course there are bigger and bigger problems in that the users of these sites allow the site owners to constitute their thoughts, and this is related to Richard Thaler’s libertarian paternalism, they make you feel that it is your decision, it is not at all. The owner has the power to choose which posts appear and which ones disappear, and he may even ban many people just because they talked about topics he didn’t like. Or, if he wants to make the majority of his users think one way he chooses, he can simply do so by erasing anything related to those who think differently that he doesn’t like so that only the herd will be left.

Personally, I usually prevent myself from falling into these traps by using rss feeds instead of reddit/facebook/twitter front-ends. rss is so simple, it doesn’t care either how many people have seen this post or how many likes it have, the first post I’ll see in my rss feeds is just the most recent post.
