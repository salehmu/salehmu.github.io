Oracle: The Next Level of Data Reporting (or Abu Noaf Forums)

October 07, 2021 • Technology, Blogs

Sometimes I ask myself why corporates crash all these disputes about whether their DBMS is that safe or not. Users are not safe in any case with a corporate, rather, the DBs (their data) are leaked anyway in the corporate's reports (which are sold to another users, the advertiser or whoever will use it), of course they won't leak your metadata but not because you really matter, actually the opposed, you don't.. your metadata are useless for anyone and they just need reports about people like you.

Anyway, I wanted to build a RDBMS infrastructure this week, which is painful task for me, not for I don't like working with databases the but I needed to run an Oracle software (I'm pretty sure that the 'suckness' and the process of making something so painful to use is a main feature in the proprietary software industry) but I got used to something like this.. It wasn't a big deal.

But, and here is a big but, Oracle had another opinion, back in the time there was what we called Abut Noaf Forum, the real front of the internet in the Middle East, if there is something I do remember very well about that infamous period of my life in which I used to visit this place as one of my top sites, it won't be all the Good Morning I7baa photos I created in my cringe PhotoShopCS setup nor gulf e-hooker who tried to virtual seduce me (a Sheikh) but: that you have to login to literally do anything includes seeing the photos I made or downloading it.

That was very annoying especially when I was outdoor with my friend and wanted to download some fancy wallpapers for his obsolete cell phone. But it wasn't a problem since there wasn't even an email verification so we used to sign up with wiqejoi@irwaojr.com. The thing here that made Abu Noaf to require us to sign up is that he wanted us to join his amazing community (which is tempting if you know what I mean). But this is not the case with the Oracle shit.

Oracle has upgraded itself to the next level of data collecting, you're not out of the game if you're a developer (actually sometimes I notice that most developers are not 'programmers' anymore, they're technologies users) the but there are VScode (Microsoft's proprietary distribution of Code text editor) and gdeveloper (and whatever JetBrains has for you) out there to spy on you too.. Facebook has facebook developer products as well.. and Oracle, well, Oracle itself spies on you.

Here what happens when you need to download (and sometimes, to read) something with the Oracle registered trademark: you have to tell Oracle who are you, what are you working for, why you need the 'thing'? what organization told you to use it and please give us its address (and patiently yours), its phone number, and of course the rest of your personal information.

Of course they store whatever they can of your fingerprints from your browser (e.g. screen size and the operating system), they don't response to my don't track flag. But, after all of this.. would you give me a good services please? No.

Exactly. You receive nothing worthful(at least if you're using freeBSD like me or any other non-normie friendly usable operating system). You receive a non-documented software, go wiled and do your best with it. Got some errors? Well connect our support. Oh I forgot, you can't. And that's the funny part: You can't even see other's solved issues to get help, the but you should tell oracle first who are you, what's your organization etc.. and no, it doesn't end up here you have to pay to 'subscribe' in a support plan, to get support in an issue in their made software. Imagine making money from your own bugs. lmao.

I feel very repent that I even thought it might be a good experience with Oracle. I gave up with learning pSQL after wasting one week trying to get Oracle works. Wish me luck.
