Philosophy

An inequilateral note about reading philosophy; the vast majority of philosophy books is not worth reading, in fact it’s painful to read it, which is a thing I realized too late (~1 year ago, thanks to a friend of mine allowed me to read his dissertation in Obscurantism of philosophers), a lot of philosophy books I’ve read in the full list was useful but not really that ‘useful’, this is not the best place to explain why, but I’ll talk about this in the feature Inshaa' Allah. Also, read How not to write in philosophy – Against obscurantism.

  • History of Political Philosophy (Strauss)

Terse book, which is a good point in reading philosophy, the briefer, the better when it comes to philosophy, but the reason behind the oversimplifying in a lot of places is first because it’s “a history” of philosophy, imagine trying to abbreviate hundreds of books in a book of two volumes, it will be inevitably oversimplified, but the main reason is that Strauss is trying to create a “Political Perspective” for a lot of philosophers that shouldn’t have a one, which make him either fall into a procrustean or make him just skip points and let it be a brief portal for each philosopher. An example of the abbreviation is Karl Marx chapter (about 30 pages), you can’t read an essay about Marx.

  • A Critical History of Greek Philosophy
  • A Theological-Political Treatise: A Political Treatise
  • In Praise of Idleness and Other Essays

A prelude of the human history from the ‘civilization’ perspective, a deep-rooted academic corresponding would be Will Durant’s first chapter of “The Full Story of Civilization”, Our Oriental Heritage. Russel’s book is good for having a good time with a book “The time you enjoy wasting is not wasted” he says.

Politics

  • The Communist Manifesto

I won’t usually recommend reading this, but I want to make sure that everyone will read it, even if you are not going to study Marxism, reading the Manifesto at least is أضعف الإيمان to prevent yourself from the odds and the ends of the anti-bolshevism propaganda (which usually includes “Marxism”). A better alternative would be Nikoalai Bukharin’s “ABC of Communism”, or even better, read Marx carefully.

  • في الثورة والقابلية للثورة، عن المثقف والثورة

Notice: these are two titles, I’d rather recommend Chomsky’s or Edward Said’s The Responsibility of the Intellectual, but the first is not a book, and I didn’t finish the last.

  • Who is Charlie?: Xenophobia and the New Middle Class (Review)

Fuck France. This books is about the laïcité (the f*ricnh secularism) and how the xenophobia works as very good engine for moving the French people towards more and more so-called ‘free speech’ movements. A very important and related book is Jean Baubérot’s La laïcité Française et ses Mutations.

  • Media Control: The Spectacular Achievements of Propaganda1(Abridgement)

This is an oversimplified Manufacturing Consent, however the last is about to be an out-of-date model, see The Propaganda Model Today by Joan Pedro-Carañana et al.

  • Discourse on Voluntary Servitude

I consider this as a very nice literary message, actually it reminds me of how the intellectual treated “crowds” in the early 19 and 20 centuries, read about this here [Arabic]. Simply, like Le Bon and Frizer did in there psychology and anthropology papers, Étienne de La Boétie is cursing the scum peoples who let tyrant governors control and torture them. Of course without giving any explanation on why they do so. It’s very obsolete to take it as an ‘intellectual’ or ‘anthropological’ approach.

Middle East Struggle Related

  • The Arabs: A History (Rogan)

If there is only one book that is neutral in the history of The Middle East, it would be this book. I recommend it for everyone needs to understand how we winded up with the 2000s pitiful middle east. Note that the books is oversimplified in the post-caliphate era.

  • Engineers of Jihad

A important literature to understand the uprising of Jihad movements between pan-Arabists, ex pan-Arabists in general, and between engineers and the middle class in particular. I think it’s even a good introduction to answer the question of why Muslims might bomb themselves. It’s one of the greatest books I’ve ever read about the armed Jihad.
