Terse book, which is a good point in reading philosophy, the briefer, the better when it comes to philosophy, but the reason behind the oversimplifying in a lot of places is first because it’s “a history” of philosophy, imagine trying to abbreviate hundreds of books in a book of two volumes, it will be inevitably oversimplified, but the main reason is that Strauss is trying to create a “Political Perspective” for a lot of philosophers that shouldn’t have a one, which make him either fall into a procrustean or make him just skip points and let it be a brief portal for each philosopher. An example of the abbreviation is Karl Marx chapter (about 30 pages), you can’t read an essay about Marx.
